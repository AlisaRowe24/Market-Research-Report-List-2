<p><p>The Continuous Peripheral Nerve Block Catheter market features three primary types. Stimulating catheters utilize electrical stimulation to locate nerves accurately before infusion, enhancing precision and efficacy. Non-stimulating catheters, in contrast, rely on anatomical landmarks and visual cues for placement, making them less reliant on technology. Over-the-needle catheters are designed to provide direct access to peripheral nerves, typically offering ease of insertion and flexibility in various clinical settings. Together, these catheters cater to diverse patient needs and procedural preferences.</p></p>
