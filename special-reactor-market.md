<p><p>The special reactor market is primarily divided into two types: non-shielded and shielded reactors. Non-shielded reactors lack additional protective barriers, making them more compact and less expensive, but they require careful handling due to radiation exposure concerns. In contrast, shielded reactors incorporate protective materials to minimize radiation leakage, ensuring safer operation and higher regulatory compliance. These reactors typically cater to applications where radiation levels must be controlled, such as in medical and research facilities, influencing their respective market demand and pricing.</p></p>
