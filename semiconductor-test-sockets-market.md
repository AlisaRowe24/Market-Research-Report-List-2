<p><p>The semiconductor test sockets market is segmented into several types based on package formats. BGA (Ball Grid Array) sockets allow for efficient testing of chips mounted on a substrate with balls of solder. QFN (Quad Flat No-lead) sockets cater to leadless packages, providing a compact design with enhanced thermal performance. WLCSP (Wafer-Level Chip Scale Package) sockets enable testing directly at the wafer level, reducing time and cost. Other types encompass various proprietary formats, catering to niche applications and emerging technologies in semiconductor testing.</p></p>
