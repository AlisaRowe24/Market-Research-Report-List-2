<p><p>The natural cosmetic colorant market encompasses three primary types: plant, animal, and microbial colorants. Plant colorants are derived from fruits, vegetables, and herbs, offering a wide range of shades and benefits. Animal colorants, sourced from sources like cochineal and carmine, provide vibrant hues but raise ethical concerns. Microbial colorants are produced through fermentation processes, utilizing microorganisms to create pigments that are often sustainable and innovative. Each type serves distinct consumer preferences, aligning with trends toward natural and ethical beauty products.</p></p>
