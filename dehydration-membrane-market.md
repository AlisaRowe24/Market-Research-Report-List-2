Gas dehydration involves the removal of water vapor from gases, often utilizing membranes that selectively separate moisture, which is crucial for optimizing gas purity and preventing corrosion. Solvent dehydration, on the other hand, relies on chemical solvents to extract water content from liquid substances, enhancing product quality in industries like food and pharmaceuticals. Both methods leverage advanced membrane technologies to improve efficiency and sustainability in dehydration processes.</p></p>
